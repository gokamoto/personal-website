June 10, 2015

Welcome to the first post of my blog! I thought I would kick things off with a sneak peek into the sort of work I do in my MSAN classes. Unfortunately, we keep a lot of our projects under wraps so that professors can reuse assignments for future cohorts. However, a few projects are open-ended enough to ease the worry of reuse, and I have added them to my github and elsewhere. Hopefully at some point in the future I'll compose a more blog-friendly summary of these projects. Enjoy!


  • San Francisco Muni Feedback Visualizations

    Completed as part of the Information Visualization course. Used D3 to create a interactive visualization dashboard that displays the San Francisco 311 reports for Muni from January-April 2015.


  • Yelp Recommendation Engine

    Completed as part of the Advanced Machine Learning course. Predicting users' star ratings of businesses using random forest. Conducted feature engineering to minimize data leakage, reduce dimensionality, and improve model performance. Feature engineering techniques included collaborative filtering, principal components analysis, and k-means.
